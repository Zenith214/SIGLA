## Getting Started

### Accessing CPAP Management

1. Log in to the PULSE system with your ADMIN credentials
2. Click on your profile menu in the top-right corner
3. Select **"CPAP Management"** from the dropdown menu
4. You'll see the CPAP Management Dashboard

### Dashboard Overview

The dashboard has two main views:

**Review Mode** (Default)
- List of all CPAPs across all barangays
- Filter by status, cycle, or barangay
- Quick access to review actions

**Monitoring Mode**
- Progress tracking for approved CPAPs
- Completion metrics and statistics
- Implementation status overview

### Understanding Your Role

As an ADMIN user, you can:
- ✅ View all CPAPs from all barangays
- ✅ Review submitted CPAPs
- ✅ Approve or request revisions
- ✅ Monitor implementation progress
- ✅ Access all survey cycles and barangays

You cannot:
- ❌ Create or edit CPAP items directly
- ❌ Update progress (only OFFICER users can)
- ❌ Delete CPAPs (system-level action only)

## Reviewing Submitted CPAPs

### Finding CPAPs to Review

#### Using Filters

1. **Status Filter**: Select "Submitted" to see CPAPs awaiting review
2. **Cycle Filter**: Focus on specific survey cycles
3. **Barangay Filter**: Review specific barangays
4. **Search**: Type barangay name for quick access

#### Priority Indicators

CPAPs are sorted by:
- Submission date (oldest first by default)
- Status (Submitted items appear first)
- Barangay name (alphabetical)

### Opening a CPAP for Review

1. Click on any CPAP row in the list
2. The CPAP Review Modal opens
3. Review all details before making a decision

### What to Review

#### Header Information

Check:
- **Barangay Name**: Verify correct barangay
- **Survey Cycle**: Confirm appropriate cycle
- **Submission Date**: Note how long it's been pending
- **Item Count**: Ensure sufficient action items

#### Action Items

For each item, evaluate:

**1. Priority Area**
- ✅ Clearly defined service area
- ✅ Aligned with survey feedback
- ✅ Appropriate scope for barangay
- ❌ Vague or overly broad
- ❌ Outside barangay jurisdiction

**2. Target Output**
- ✅ Specific and actionable
- ✅ Measurable deliverable
- ✅ Realistic for barangay capacity
- ❌ Vague or generic
- ❌ Unrealistic or overly ambitious

**3. Success Indicator**
- ✅ Quantifiable metric
- ✅ Achievable target
- ✅ Relevant to target output
- ❌ Unmeasurable
- ❌ Disconnected from output

**4. Responsible Person**
- ✅ Named individual or specific position
- ✅ Appropriate authority level
- ✅ Realistic assignment
- ❌ Generic role (e.g., "staff")
- ❌ Missing or unclear

**5. Timeline**
- ✅ Realistic duration
- ✅ Start date after approval
- ✅ End date before next cycle
- ❌ Too short for complexity
- ❌ Extends beyond reasonable period

### Quality Criteria

#### Excellent CPAP
- 5-10 well-defined action items
- Clear alignment with survey data
- Specific, measurable outputs
- Realistic timelines
- Named responsible persons
- Mix of short, medium, and long-term actions
- Addresses multiple service areas

#### Acceptable CPAP
- 3-5 action items
- Generally aligned with priorities
- Mostly specific outputs
- Reasonable timelines
- Identifiable responsible parties
- Covers key service areas

#### Needs Revision
- Fewer than 3 items or too many (>15)
- Vague or generic outputs
- Unmeasurable indicators
- Unrealistic timelines
- Missing responsible persons
- Poor alignment with survey data
- Items outside barangay authority

## Approving CPAPs

### When to Approve

Approve a CPAP when:
- ✅ All items meet quality criteria
- ✅ Outputs are specific and measurable
- ✅ Timelines are realistic
- ✅ Responsible persons are named
- ✅ Plan addresses key priorities
- ✅ Items are within barangay authority

### Approval Process

1. Review all action items thoroughly
2. Click the **"Approve"** button
3. (Optional) Add approval comments
4. Confirm your decision
5. The OFFICER will be notified immediately

### Approval Comments (Optional)

Use approval comments to:
- Commend strong elements
- Provide encouragement
- Offer suggestions for future cycles
- Highlight best practices

**Example:**
```
"Excellent CPAP with clear, measurable outputs. The mobile health clinic 
initiative is particularly well-planned. Consider adding a water quality 
testing component in the next cycle."
```

### What Happens After Approval

- CPAP status changes to **Approved**
- OFFICER receives notification
- OFFICER can now update progress
- CPAP appears in Monitoring view
- Most fields become read-only for OFFICER

## Requesting Revisions

### When to Request Revisions

Request revisions when:
- ❌ Items are too vague or generic
- ❌ Success indicators are unmeasurable
- ❌ Timelines are unrealistic
- ❌ Responsible persons are missing
- ❌ Items are outside barangay authority
- ❌ Plan doesn't address key priorities
- ❌ Insufficient number of items

### Revision Request Process

1. Review the CPAP and identify issues
2. Click the **"Request Revision"** button
3. **Write detailed comments** (required)
4. Confirm your decision
5. The OFFICER will be notified with your comments

### Writing Effective Revision Comments

#### Be Specific

❌ **Poor:** "Items need improvement"

✅ **Good:** "Item 2: Please specify which health services will be provided 
in the mobile clinic. Item 4: The timeline of 2 weeks is too short for 
infrastructure work - suggest 3-6 months."

#### Be Constructive

❌ **Poor:** "This CPAP is inadequate"

✅ **Good:** "Good start on identifying priorities. To strengthen this plan, 
please add specific target numbers to your success indicators and name the 
actual staff members who will lead each initiative."

#### Provide Guidance

❌ **Poor:** "Fix the timelines"

✅ **Good:** "Consider adjusting timelines: Information campaigns can be 
completed in 1-3 months, but infrastructure projects typically need 6-12 
months. Review each item's complexity and adjust accordingly."

#### Prioritize Issues

Structure your comments:
1. **Critical issues** (must fix)
2. **Important improvements** (should fix)
3. **Suggestions** (nice to have)

**Example:**
```
CRITICAL:
- Item 3 is missing a responsible person - please assign
- Item 5 success indicator is not measurable - needs specific metric

IMPORTANT:
- Items 1 and 2 have very similar outputs - consider combining
- Timeline for Item 4 seems too short (2 weeks for construction)

SUGGESTIONS:
- Consider adding an action item for water quality based on survey feedback
- Item 6 could benefit from partnership with the municipal health office
```

### Common Revision Reasons

| Issue | Example | Guidance |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Vague output | "Improve health services" | "Specify what will be improved and how" |
| Unmeasurable indicator | "Better satisfaction" | "Use specific percentage or number target" |
| Missing responsible person | "Barangay staff" | "Name the specific person or position" |
| Unrealistic timeline | "Build health center in 1 month" | "Infrastructure projects need 6-12 months" |
| Outside authority | "Build provincial road" | "Focus on barangay-level interventions" |
| Too few items | Only 1-2 items | "Add more items to address other priorities" |

### What Happens After Revision Request

- CPAP status changes to **Revision Requested**
- OFFICER receives notification with your comments
- OFFICER can edit all fields
- OFFICER will resubmit when ready
- You'll review the revised version

## Monitoring Progress

### Accessing Monitoring View

1. Click the **"Monitoring"** tab in the dashboard
2. View all approved CPAPs
3. See progress indicators and statistics

### Monitoring Dashboard Features

#### Summary Cards

- **Total Approved CPAPs**: Count across all barangays
- **Average Completion**: Overall progress percentage
- **On Track**: CPAPs meeting timeline expectations
- **Needs Attention**: CPAPs with delays or issues

#### CPAP List

For each approved CPAP:
- Barangay name
- Approval date
- Number of items
- Completion percentage
- Last update timestamp
- Status indicator (On Track / Behind / Completed)

### Viewing Detailed Progress

1. Click on any CPAP in the monitoring list
2. View all action items with progress details
3. Review:
   - Original target output
   - Actual output achieved
   - Accomplishment status
   - OFFICER remarks
   - Timeline vs. actual progress

### Progress Indicators

**Item Status:**
- 🟢 **Completed**: Target achieved
- 🟡 **In Progress**: On track
- 🟠 **Delayed**: Behind schedule
- 🔴 **Not Started**: No progress yet

**Overall CPAP Status:**
- **On Track**: ≥80% of items progressing as planned
- **Behind Schedule**: 50-79% on track
- **Needs Attention**: <50% on track

### Monitoring Best Practices

#### Regular Review

- Check monitoring dashboard weekly
- Identify CPAPs needing attention
- Track trends across barangays
- Note best practices for sharing

#### Proactive Support

When you notice issues:
- Contact the OFFICER directly
- Offer technical assistance
- Connect them with resources
- Facilitate inter-barangay learning

#### Documentation

- Take notes on implementation challenges
- Document successful strategies
- Track common obstacles
- Prepare reports for leadership

### Generating Reports

#### Monthly Progress Report

Include:
- Number of approved CPAPs
- Overall completion rates
- Top-performing barangays
- Common challenges
- Success stories

#### Cycle-End Report

Include:
- Total CPAPs submitted and approved
- Completion statistics
- Impact assessment
- Lessons learned
- Recommendations for next cycle

## Best Practices

### Review Efficiency

**Batch Processing**
- Set aside dedicated review time
- Review multiple CPAPs in one session
- Use consistent evaluation criteria
- Take breaks to maintain quality

**Quick Assessment**
- Scan all items first for overview
- Flag obvious issues immediately
- Deep-dive on borderline cases
- Make decisive judgments

**Time Management**
- Target 10-15 minutes per CPAP review
- Don't over-analyze acceptable plans
- Focus revision comments on critical issues
- Approve good-enough plans promptly

### Quality Assurance

**Consistency**
- Apply same standards to all barangays
- Document your evaluation criteria
- Review your past decisions periodically
- Calibrate with other admins

**Fairness**
- Don't penalize for minor issues
- Focus on substance over style
- Consider barangay capacity differences
- Provide equal support to all

**Continuous Improvement**
- Learn from approved CPAPs that succeed
- Identify patterns in revision requests
- Refine your evaluation criteria
- Share insights with other admins

### Communication

**Timely Responses**
- Review submissions within 3-5 business days
- Respond to OFFICER questions promptly
- Provide status updates if delayed
- Set clear expectations

**Clear Feedback**
- Use simple, direct language
- Provide specific examples
- Explain the "why" behind requests
- Offer constructive suggestions

**Supportive Tone**
- Acknowledge effort and strengths
- Frame revisions as improvements
- Encourage innovation
- Celebrate successes

### Monitoring Excellence

**Proactive Engagement**
- Don't wait for problems to escalate
- Reach out to struggling barangays
- Share success stories
- Facilitate peer learning

**Data-Driven Decisions**
- Track completion rates
- Identify common obstacles
- Measure impact where possible
- Use data to improve the process

**Continuous Support**
- Provide technical assistance
- Connect barangays with resources
- Facilitate partnerships
- Remove implementation barriers

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

#### "I can't see any CPAPs"

**Possible Causes:**
- No CPAPs have been submitted yet
- Filters are too restrictive
- System access issue

**Solutions:**
- Check filter settings (reset to "All")
- Verify survey cycle is active
- Contact technical support if issue persists

#### "CPAP won't approve"

**Possible Causes:**
- Network error
- System validation failure
- Session timeout

**Solutions:**
- Check internet connection
- Refresh the page and try again
- Log out and log back in
- Contact technical support

#### "Revision comments not saving"

**Solutions:**
- Ensure comments field is not empty
- Check character limit (max 5000 characters)
- Try shorter comments
- Copy comments to clipboard before submitting

#### "Progress data not updating"

**Possible Causes:**
- OFFICER hasn't updated yet
- Cache issue
- Display lag

**Solutions:**
- Check "Last Updated" timestamp
- Refresh the page
- Clear browser cache
- Verify with OFFICER directly

### Getting Help

**Technical Issues:**
- Email: support@pulse.gov.ph
- Include: Your username, CPAP ID, error message

**Policy Questions:**
- Contact: DILG Regional Office
- Reference: CPAP Implementation Guidelines

**Training Needs:**
- Request: Admin training session
- Topics: Review standards, monitoring techniques

## Appendix: Review Checklist

### Pre-Review

- [ ] Verify barangay and cycle information
- [ ] Check submission date
- [ ] Note number of action items
- [ ] Review any previous submissions from this barangay

### Item-by-Item Review

For each action item:

- [ ] Priority area is clear and specific
- [ ] Target output is actionable and measurable
- [ ] Success indicator is quantifiable
- [ ] Responsible person is named
- [ ] Timeline start is after approval date
- [ ] Timeline end is before next cycle
- [ ] Item is within barangay authority
- [ ] Item aligns with survey priorities

### Overall Assessment

- [ ] Sufficient number of items (3-10 recommended)
- [ ] Mix of short, medium, long-term actions
- [ ] Addresses multiple service areas
- [ ] Realistic given barangay capacity
- [ ] Clear alignment with survey data
- [ ] No duplicate or overlapping items

### Decision

- [ ] **Approve**: All criteria met, ready for implementation
- [ ] **Request Revision**: Specific issues identified and documented
